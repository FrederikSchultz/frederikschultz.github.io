Thesis

In this paper the effect of tagging of Narwhals from East Greenland is examined by looking at different models that have a time since tagging component that goes towards zero. A Kolmogorov Smirnow test has been conducted, and found that all of the models suggested in this thesis can be rejected, but by looking at other articles it is assumed that if memory was included in the models, some of these models might not be rejectable for all of the whales. A mediation analysis was conducted, which showed that a handling time that takes more than 63 minutes results in the highest proportion, and a handling time between 55 and 62 minutes, the lowest. Lastly we have examined how long it takes before the whales' buzzing rate is back to natural, and found that this also depends on the handling time.
